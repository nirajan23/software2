A Thangka painting shop showcases traditional Tibetan and Nepali art, featuring vibrant, handcrafted Thangka paintings. These detailed works depict deities, mandalas, and spiritual symbols, each created using natural pigments on cotton or silk. Visitors can often see artists at work and learn about the spiritual meanings behind each piece from knowledgeable shopkeepers. Some shops also offer workshops or live demonstrations, making it a culturally immersive experience with related artifacts like statues and prayer flags.

GREEN TARA

Green Tara, also known as Shyama Tara, is a cherished figure in Tibetan Buddhism. With her green hue and compassionate nature, she is revered for helping devotees remove obstacles and achieve their aspirations. Seated on a lotus with one leg extended, she embodies readiness to aid those in need.

SARASWOTI

Saraswati is a significant figure in Hindu mythology, often depicted as a beautiful woman dressed in white, symbolizing purity. She is usually shown with a veena (a musical instrument), a book, and a rosary, embodying the arts, wisdom, and education. Celebrated during the festival of Vasant Panchami, devotees seek her blessings for knowledge and skill.

BHAIRAB

Bhairav, also known simply as Bhairav, is a fierce manifestation of Lord Shiva in Hindu mythology. He is often associated with time, destruction, and the protection of the universe from evil forces. Kaal Bhairav is typically depicted as a dark-skinned figure adorned with a garland of skulls, holding various weapons, and accompanied by dogs, which are considered sacred in this context. This painting is particularly influenced by Newari culture from the Kathmandu Valley.
